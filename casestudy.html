Case Study

AirBnB in Amsterdam, 2019

CareerFoundry: Data Analytics Immersion
January 14, 2025

View the complete case study here.

Analysis of AirBnB listing and review data from Amsterdam for the year 2019, retrieved from Kaggle.
This study uncovers insights regarding the distribution of rental prices, accommodation types, availability and user ratings across neighbourhoods, as well as how these variables change throughout the year.
With these insights, this study aims to inform visitors to the city, as well as residents hosting accommodations, about the dynamics of the short-term rental market.

Tools Used: Python (pandas, NumPy, SciKitLearn, statsmodels, Matplotlib, Seaborn, folium), Tableau Public


A map showing some of the least-popular listings in central Amsterdam in 2019, with 10 days or less booked for the year.

More examples of visualizations created during the analysis: a map showing the average listing price by neighbourhood (left) and a pie chart showing the distribution of room types across the whole city (right)
